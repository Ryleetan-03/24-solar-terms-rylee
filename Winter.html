Beginning of Winter

It is one of the most important seasonal points in Chinese folklore, where spring is plowed and summer is cultivated, autumn is harvested and winter is hidden, and winter is the season to enjoy the harvest and recuperation. In ancient times, it was also one of the "four seasons and eight festivals" and was a very important festival, with ancestor worship and feasting in some parts of China. Traditionally, winter starts at the beginning of the twenty-four solar terms "Lidong", which means that everything enters a state of rest and recuperation.

Light Snow

Xiaoxue, the 20th of the twenty-four solar terms, is the second solar term in winter, occurring on November 22 or 23 of each year, when the sun reaches 240° of the ecliptic. Xiaoxue is a festival that reflects precipitation and temperature, and it is a festival with a high frequency of cold wave and strong cold air activity. The arrival of the Little Snow Festival means that the weather will become colder and the amount of precipitation will gradually increase. Snow falls, the coming year rain evenly, no big drought and flood; two, snow can freeze some germs and pests, the coming year to reduce the occurrence of pests and diseases; three, snow has a warming effect, conducive to the decomposition of organic matter in the soil, enhance soil fertility

heavy snow

“Heavy “Snow is a termthat reflects the characteristics of the climate. It is characterized by a significant drop in temperature and an increase in precipitation compared to autumn and early winter. At the time of snow, most areas of China have entered the cold winter season, with the lowest temperatures in some northern regions falling to 0°C or below. Precipitation (rain or snow) increases in areas where cold and warm air meet in front of strong cold air. In meteorology, water vapour condensation such as rain, snow and hail that falls from the sky to the ground is called "precipitation". The meaning of the seasonal snow is different from that of the weather snow. In fact, the snow during Heavy Snowis often not as big as the Light Snow, and the largest amount of snow throughout the year is not in the Light Snow and Heavy Snow time.

Winter Solstice

Winter Solstice, also known as the Southern Solstice, the Winter Festival, and the Sub-years, has both natural and humanistic connotations, and is not only an important festival among the 24 solar terms, but also a traditional ancestral festival among Chinese folk. The winter solstice is one of the eight festivals of the four seasons, and is considered a major winter festival, and in ancient times it was said that "the winter solstice is as important as the New Year". Winter solstice customs vary in content or details depending on the region. In southern China, there is a custom of ancestor worship and feasting on the winter solstice. In northern China, it is customary to eat dumplings on the winter solstice.
