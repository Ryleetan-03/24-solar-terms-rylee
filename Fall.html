beginning of Autumn

Beginning of Autumn is the thirteenth of the twenty-four solar terms, and the start of autumn. It is the third hottest of the twenty-four solar terms, after the summer heat and the summer heat. It means that precipitation and humidity are at a turning point in the year, tending to decline or decrease. Autumn is the season of ripening and harvesting of grains and cereals. In the ancient times, it was customary for folk to worship the god of the land and celebrate the harvest at the time of autumn.

end of heat

The fourteenth of the twenty-four solar terms, end of the heat. At the end of heat, it is the "last summer" of the "three summer heat", which means that the hot weather has come to an end. Temperatures have begun to generally trend down.

White Dew

When it comes to White Dew, summer winds are gradually replaced by winter winds, cold air turns to attack, plus the direct sunlight point moves south, the sunshine time in the northern hemisphere becomes shorter, the intensity of light is weakened, the ground radiation heat dissipation is fast, so the temperature drop is also gradually accelerated. The white dew basically ends the stifling heat of the summer days, the weather gradually turns cooler, and the cold produces dew condensation. The ancients used the four seasons to match the five elements, autumn is gold, golden white, white to describe the autumn dew, so the name "white dew"

Autumnal Equinox

Autumn Equinox is the sixteenth of the twenty-four solar terms and the fourth of the autumnal season. Autumnal equinox, the sun almost directly on the earth's equator, the global day and night equal length . The autumnal equinox, " divided" means "equal", "half", in addition to referring to the equal division of day and night, there is a layer of meaning is equal to the autumn. After the autumnal equinox, the direct sunlight position shifts southward, the day is short and the night is long in the northern hemisphere, the temperature difference between day and night increases, and the temperature drops day by day

Cold Dew

Cold Dew is the seventeenth of the twenty-four solar terms. It is a festival that reflects the characteristics of climate change. As we enter the cold dew, there is cold air coming south, a big temperature difference between day and night, and an obvious autumn dryness. In terms of climate characteristics, during the cold dew season, the south is becoming more autumnal, cool and windy, with little rain and dryness; the north has shown winter scenes in most areas. The traditional customs of cold dew include enjoying maple leaves, eating sesame, eating crabs, drinking tea, etc.

First Frost

First frost, the eighteenth of the twenty-four solar terms, is the last festival of autumn. First Frost does not mean "frost", but a sudden drop in temperature and a large temperature difference between day and night. On average, First Frost is the time of the year when the temperature difference between day and night is the greatest.
